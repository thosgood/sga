SGA 6: Intersection theory and the Riemann–Roch theorem

IV. Grothendieck groups of ringed toposes

1. Reminders and generalities on Grothendieck groups

Let {\mathcal{C}} be a triangulated category. Recall (I 6.3 and [SGA 5 VIII 2]) that a map f from \operatorname{ob}{\mathcal{C}} to an abelian group G is said to be additive if we have f(E)=f(E')+f(E'') for every distinguished triangle E'\to E\to E''\to E'[1]. The additive maps from \operatorname{ob}{\mathcal{C}} to G form an abelian group, which depends functorially on G. The functor thus obtained is represented by an abelian group k({\mathcal{C}}) and a universal additive map \operatorname{cl}\colon\operatorname{ob}{\mathcal{C}}\to k({\mathcal{C}}) (denoted by \operatorname{cl} when there is no risk of confusion).

The group k({\mathcal{C}}) depends functorially on {\mathcal{C}} with respect to exact functors. If {\mathcal{C}} and {\mathcal{C}}' are triangulated categories, then any two isomorphic exact functors from {\mathcal{C}} to {\mathcal{C}}' induce the same homomorphism k({\mathcal{C}})\to k({\mathcal{C}}'); in particular, if u\colon{\mathcal{C}}\to{\mathcal{C}}' is an exact functor that is an equivalence of categories, then k(u)\colon k({\mathcal{C}})\to k({\mathcal{C}}') is an isomorphism.

Let {\mathcal{C}} be a triangulated category, and L\in\operatorname{ob}{\mathcal{C}}.

  1. For every n\in\mathbb{Z}, we have \operatorname{cl}(L[n]) = (-1)^n\operatorname{cl}(L). If L' and L'' are objects of {\mathcal{C}} such that we have L\cong L'\oplus L'', then we have \operatorname{cl}(L) = \operatorname{cl}(L') + \operatorname{cl}(L'').
  2. Suppose that there exists n\in\mathbb{Z} such that \coprod_{i\geqslant 0}L[2ni] is representable. Then \operatorname{cl}(L)=0.

Proof. Claim (a) follows directly from the definitions (cf. [SGA 5 VIII 2]). For (b), it suffices to remark that we have \coprod_{i\geqslant 0} L[2ni] \cong L\oplus\left(\coprod_{i\geqslant 0} L[2ni]\right)[2n] and to apply (a).

Let {\mathcal{C}} be a triangulated category, and {\mathcal{A}} a thick subcategory ([@IV-V, I 2.1]). Then the inclusion and passage to the quotient functors define an exact sequence k({\mathcal{A}}) \to k({\mathcal{C}}) \to k({\mathcal{C}}/{\mathcal{A}}) \to 0.

Proof. See [SGA 5 VIII 3.1].

Let {\mathcal{A}} be an additive (resp. abelian) category, and f an additive function (1.1) on \operatorname{ob}\operatorname{K}^\mathrm{b}(A) (resp. \operatorname{D}^\mathrm{b}(A)). For E\in\operatorname{ob}\operatorname{K}^\mathrm{b}(A) (resp. \operatorname{D}^\mathrm{b}(A)) we have f(E) = \sum (-1)^i f(E^i) (resp. f(E)=\sum(-1)^i f(\operatorname{H}^i(E))).

Proof. Left as an exercise to the reader.
